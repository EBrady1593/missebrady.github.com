History

In the 15th century, the French explored and colonized Canada. It was claimed in 1534 with permanent settlements starting in 1608. After the loss of the Seven Years' War, France in 1763 handed over almost all North American assets to Britain. The present British province of Quebec was divided into Upper Canada and Lower Canada in 1791 and merged in 1841. In 1867, the Canadian province merged with two other British colonies, New Brunswick and Nova Scotia, to form Canada. The new country was expanded by the incorporation of the rest of North America, and in 1949 formed an alliance with Newfoundland and Labrador.


Cities

Some major cities in Canada include Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver, Calgary, and Edmonton.
