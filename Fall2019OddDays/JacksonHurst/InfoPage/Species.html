Species of Bearded dragons

Eastern Bearded Dragon

Eastern bearded dragons often grow between 24 inches and 20 inches in overall lenght.

They are also reconized by their iconic beard, which they puff up when they feel stressed or threatened.

Rankins Bearded Dragon

The Rankin's bearded dragon is similar to the larger central beared dragon, but the Rankin's is smaller, usually less the 12 inches in overall lenght.

Another way to tell this dragon from it's similar looking cousins is that the Rankin's dragon has a more blunt snout.

Western Bearded Dragon

Western bearded dragons are quite large, growing to 38 centimeters in lenght.

They also can be reconized by their smaller heads and spines arranged on their underside and neck.

Central Bearded Dragon

Central beared dragons can grow up to 24 in in lenght.

Central bearded dragon eggs can switch genders based on the tempature the are incubated in.
