History

North Korea is a country in East Asia consitituting the northern part of the Korean Peninsula. North Korea officially describes itself as a "self-reliant" socialist state, and formally holds elections. in 1945, Korea was divided into two zones, with the north occupied by the Soviet Union and the south occupied by the United States. To the north and northwest, the country is bordered by China and by Russia along the Amnok (known as the Yalu in Chinese) and Tumen rivers and to the south it is bordered by South Korea, with the heavily fortified Korean Demilitarized Zone (DMZ) separating the two. The president is Kim Jong-un.

    • Geography

    Located in the continent of Asia, North Korea covers 120,408 square kilometers of land and 130 square kilometers of water, making it the 99th largest nation in the world with a total area of 120,538 square kilometers.
