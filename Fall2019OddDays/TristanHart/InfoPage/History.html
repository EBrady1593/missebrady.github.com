What Caused the Hunger Games?

The story is set in post-apocalyptic North America. Natural disaster plagued the world, droughts, storms, fires, and encroaching seas that eroded the land. There was constant war over the little resources left in the land. Out of all of it came Panem, The beautiful Capitol surrounded by the thirteen districts which provided and manufactured goods. Then, in what was called the Dark Days, the districts rebelled against the Capitol. Tweleve of the districts were deafeated and the thirteenth was destroyed.

The document the Treaty of Treason was born. This treaty gave new laws to prevent any uprising and to guarentee peace. As a reminder that the Dark Days should not be repeated, the Hunger Games were born.
