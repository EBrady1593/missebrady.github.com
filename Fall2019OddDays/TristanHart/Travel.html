History

The history of Greenland dates back to the year 2500 BC, it is thought that some of the first humans arrived there. However, due to the harsh climate all their decendents died out. Greenland was rediscovered in the 10th century, 3,400 years later by Icelandoc Vikings. In the year 1200 AD, the ancestors of the mordern Inuit Greenlanders migrated there. while the Inuit surived the icy world the early Norse settlers left leaving the Inuit as the only occupants for many centuries.

Fast-forward to the year 1721, Denmark-Norway aspired to be a colonial power. They sent a missionary expedition to Greenland with the stated aim of reinstating Christanity to what they thought were the descendants of the Norse Greenalnders. When the missionaries arrived they only found Inuit Greenlanders whom they baptized. Denmark-Norway developed trading colonies along the coast and imposed a trade monopoly.

After World War II, Denmark converted Greenland's status from colony to country. Greenland is still a part of the Kingdom of Denmark but, enjoys home rule.

Geography

Greenland is Located in the western Hemisphere. To its right is its much smaller counter part, Iceland. The two are often confused with one another because of Greenland being full of snow and Iceland with many pastures. Greenland is in the North Atlantic Ocean, east of Canada, and in the Arctic Circle.

Climate: The air qualtiy in Greenland is among the best in the world due to where it is positioned geographically. There is very low amounts of humidity in Greenland's Dry climate. However, Greenland is quite cold. Event the southern most part of the country never exceeds 68Â° F.
