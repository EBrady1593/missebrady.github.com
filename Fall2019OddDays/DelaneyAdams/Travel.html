Geography

Greece is a country located in Southern Europe. Greece is considered to be the most mountainous country in Europe, most of the land being 80% mountains or hills. Mount Olympus in Greece stands to be 9,573 feet tall, the highest peak in the country. The Pindus is a mountain range that is 8,652 feet tall. This mountain range used to serve its purpose as a barrier for East to West travel. Greece also features many islands. Greece's coastline spans to be 8,498 miles long, making it the 11th longest coastline in the world. For climate, Greece experiences wet winters and dry summers.

History

Evidence of human ancestors was found in the Petralona Cave, located in Macedonia. Greece was the place where Western civilization first began, starting with three civilized groups, the Mycenaean civilization, the Minoan civilization, and the Cycladic civilization. Greece was the first to create a democratic government system, starting in Athens, the capital of present-day Greece. Once Greece emerged from the Dark Ages, many city-states developed.
