Different Styles

J-Fashion contains many styles. Each style is different, with its own trends and people who wear it! The next pages contain information about a few J-Fashion styles!

Lolita

The lolita style is inspired by Victorian and Edwardian fashion. Lolita can be represented in many forms! There are classic lolita styles, hime lolita styles, punk lolita styles, sweet lolita styles, and more. Each form of lolita looks different from the last, although they all maintain a look similar to that of Victorian eras. Lolita styles almost always feature puffy petticoats and blouses. Lolita fashion is meant to be more modest, so people who wear lolita styles tend to cover most of their skin. Lolita fashion was popularized in the late 90's.

Decora

Another popular J-Fashion style is called decora! This style is extraordinarily different from the lolita style! Decora incorporates bright colors and childlike aspects into its style! Decora emerged out of Japan in the late 90's with lots of hair clips, clothing layers, cute stickers, and plush toys! Decora is a very extravagant, eye-catching style. Many people say that decora makes them feel really happy and welcomed into a kind community of fashion!

Fairy Kei

Fairy kei is similar to decora, but it uses pastel colors instead! Many people who wear fairy kei incorporate baggy t-shirts, cute cartoon characters, fluffy skirts, pastel sweaters, and fun accessories into their looks! Fairy kei is a similar style to decora, but it doesn't include bright colors! Fairy kei is meant to be a very sweet, pastel style, hence the name fairy kei.
