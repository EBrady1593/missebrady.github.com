J-Fashion History

J-Fashion didn't begin all at once. It took years for new styles to appear and work their way into the category of J-Fashion. Most J-Fashion styles began in the 90's and became popular in the early 2000's. Less and less people were wearing J-Fashion by the time 2010 rolled around, though. By this point, mainstream fashion was establishing itself. Less people wanted to stand out, and more wanted to go with the typical clothes that mainstream companies were offering. Even now, J-Fashion isn't that big in our society, but once you get into it, you'll find a community of kind, creative people. There are still lots of J-Fashion brands for the people who really find joy in empowering, cute clothes.
