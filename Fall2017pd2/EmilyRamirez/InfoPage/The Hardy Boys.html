This is the Nancy Drew and the Hardy Boys fan page. The links connect to information about Nancy Drew, the Hardy Boys, and the Hardy Boys/Nancy Drew Mysteries show. Both series of these books were written in the 1930's. The writer of the original Nancy Drew books wrote under the pseudonym Carolyn Keene, and the writer of the original Hardy Boys series wrote under the name Franklin W. Dixon.


The Hardy Boys

This part of the webpage is dedicated to the Hardy Boys, the detective brothers. The Hardy Boys live in Bayport in Barmet Bay, near the Atlantic Ocean. Their father is Fenton Hardy, who is a detective also. Their mother isn't mentioned as often but lives with them, and their Aunt Gertrude visits a lot, trying to keep the boys out of trouble. Some of their friends are Chet Morton, Biff Hooper, and Tony Prito. Joe's girlfriend is Iola Morton, Chet's sister, and Callie Shaw is Frank's girlfriend.

Frank is the older brother, who is 18 years old. He is very intelligent and responsible, but his brain instantly vanishes when he gets around girls. He is the wiser one of the brothers and is always prepared. Frank is described as having brown hair.

Joe is the younger brother, who is 17 years old. He is intelligent, but gets distracted easily and cares a lot about having fun, which gets him off-track sometimes. He is the goofier of the brothers and has an amazing sense of humor. Joe is described as having blond hair.


The Mystery of the Applegate Treasure and The Mystery of Ghost Farm were two serial series aired in about 1957, in which Frank and Joe are portrayed as younger: about 13.



Tommy Kirk played Joe in these series.



Tim Considine played Frank in these series.


The Hardy Boys Animated Series aired in the from 1969 to 1971.


Parker Stevenson, left, played Frank Hardy, and Shaun Cassidy, right, played Joe Hardy in the Hardy Boys Nancy Drew Mysteries in 1977.

The original Hardy Boys series was written in about 1930. This was the first book in the series.

The Hardy Boys Digest was a continuation of the original series, beginning in 1979.

The Hardy Boys Casefiles began in the late 1980's and ran the same time that the Nancy Drew Files had.

The Clues Brothers and the Secret Files were written for a younger audience, portraying Frank and Joe as younger children.


The Hardy Boys Undercover Brothers series was published at about the same time that the Nancy Drew Girl Detective Series was.

The Hardy Boys Adventures series replaced the Undercover Brothers Series as the Nancy Drew Diaries replaced the Girl Detective Series.
