History

Columbia was founded by the Spaniards in July 20, 1810. In 1536 a general advance toward the interior began independently from three different directions, under Jiménez de Quesada, Sebastián de Benalcázar (known in Colombia as Belalcázar) and Nikolaus Federmann. But the earliest known inhabitants of Columbia were Mesoamericans whom migrated from the north. Spanish soldiers tried to reclaim control in 1815, but Colombian forces led by the famed Venezuelan general Simón Bolívar defeated the Spanish in 1819.

Geography

The Republic of Colombia is largely situated in the northwest of South America, with some territories falling within the boundaries of Central America. Near the Ecuadorian frontier, the Andes Mountains divide into three distinct, roughly parallel chains, called cordilleras, that extend northeastward almost to the Caribbean Sea.

It is bordered by Panama, Venezuela, Brazil, Ecuador, and Peru. Colombia has a land size of 1,138,910 km2 (439,740 sq mi) and it is the 25th largest nation in the world and the fourth-largest country in South America.

Climate: warm, tropical weather, sometimes rainy seasons, and some cold areas.

Terrain: flat coastal lowlands, central highlands, eastern lowland plains.
