History

The Bahamas is an archipelago, a group of islands, that is located on the Northwestern Edge of the west Indies. The Bhamas was once a British colonoy but The Bahamas became independent within the comonwealth in 1973. The name Bahamas is of Lucayan Taino derivation, but some believe it is from Spanish Bajamar, meaning "shallow water." A fun fact is that Christopher Columbus landed on various Caribbean islands as his original landfall and the islands he made landfall at are now the Bahamas. Tha Bahamas are located in Northwestern Edge of the West Indies. The Bahama islands are surrounded by the Atlantic Ocean.

Climate: low 70s during the winter, to low 80s in the summer, there is about 44 inches of annual rainfall.
