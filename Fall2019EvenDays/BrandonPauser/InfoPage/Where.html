Where They Happen

Tornadoes have been reported on every single continent in the world except for Antarctica. They ca occur at any time of the year and any time of the day. Peak tornado season is from March through May. Most tornadoes occur between the hours of 3 p.m. and 9 p.m. during the day. The US has the highest likely hood of tornadoes a year and have the most reported annually, the US sees over 1000 reported tornadoes a year and Canada comes in second with around 100 a year. Some places in the world have an increased likelihood of tornadoes is most of the united states, Another hot spot is in Argentina and Uruguay. Also The United Kingdom, Russia, and Europe are another hot spot for twisters, South Africa is a hot spot as well, India and Bangladesh have an increased likeihood of tornadoes too, some other hot spots are China, Japan, Phillippines, parts of Australia, and New Zealand.

Tornado Alley

The most well known hot spot around the world is tornado alley. This area contains much of northern Texas northward through Oklahoma, Kansas, Missouri and parts of Louisiana, Iowa, Nebraska and eastern Colorado. This place is unlike any other place in the US, it is most prone to tornadoes because of air masses frequemtly colliding to produce strong and violent thunderstorms which in turn sets the stage for tornadoes.

Here is why tornadoes occur so often in tornado alley because of all the different air masses coming together in tornado alley.
